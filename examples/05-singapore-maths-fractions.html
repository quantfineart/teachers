Lesson 1 — Adding Unlike Fractions: Concept Introduction

Monday | 50 minutes | Objective: Add two unlike fractions by finding a common denominator

Concrete (10 min)

Fraction strips and discs. Students physically combine 1/3 + 1/6 using manipulatives. "Can we just put them together? Why not?" Discovery: they need same-sized pieces. Students find the equivalent fraction using the strips, then combine.

Pictorial (15 min)

Bar models. Draw fraction bars, shade and compare. Model finding LCD through visual alignment — show how 1/3 becomes 2/6 by drawing the subdivisions. Textbook pp. 62-63. Students sketch in exercise books alongside teacher demonstration.

Abstract (15 min)

Algorithm: find LCD, convert both fractions, add numerators, simplify. Worked examples on board: 1/4 + 1/6, 2/5 + 1/3, 3/8 + 1/4. Students copy method into exercise books, then try 2 examples independently.

Practice (10 min)

Textbook Exercise 4A, Q1-6 (in class). Teacher circulates, checks for common errors: forgetting to find LCD, not simplifying.

Homework

Workbook 5A, pp. 45-46

Lesson 2 — Adding Unlike Fractions: Fluency Building

Tuesday | 50 minutes | Objective: Add unlike fractions fluently, including simplifying answers

Review (5 min)

Mark homework together. Address common errors on the board — especially students who found a common denominator but not the LCD (e.g., using 24 instead of 12 for thirds and quarters).

Guided Practice (15 min)

Harder examples with larger denominators: 3/8 + 5/12, 2/9 + 5/6. Emphasise finding LCM efficiently. Bar models for the first example, then transition to abstract only.

Word Problems (20 min)

Model-drawing approach for fraction word problems:

"Sarah ate 1/4 of a cake. Her brother ate 1/3 of the same cake. What fraction of the cake did they eat altogether?"

Method: Read → Draw bar model → Identify operation → Write equation → Solve → Check answer. Students practise with 3 word problems.

Independent Practice (10 min)

Textbook Exercise 4B, Q1-8

Homework

Worksheet 4.2 (teacher-prepared — mixed addition with word problems)

Lesson 3 — Subtracting Unlike Fractions

Wednesday | 50 minutes | Objective: Subtract unlike fractions using common denominators

Concrete / Pictorial (10 min)

Fraction strips: show 3/4 - 1/3 physically. Then draw the bar model. "Same idea as adding — we need the same-sized pieces first. Then we can take away."

Abstract (15 min)

Link to addition method — same LCD process, subtract numerators instead. Worked examples including simplifying and converting improper fractions to mixed numbers: 5/6 - 1/4, 7/8 - 2/3.

Guided Practice (15 min)

Students work in pairs on mixed examples. Teacher circulates, targets weaker students for 1:1 support. Stronger students move to extension: subtract from whole numbers (e.g., 2 - 3/5).

Independent Practice (10 min)

Textbook Exercise 4C, Q1-8

Homework

Workbook 5A, pp. 47-48

Lesson 4 — Mixed Operations and Word Problems

Thursday | 50 minutes | Objective: Solve multi-step word problems involving +/- of unlike fractions

Quick-Fire Review (5 min)

Oral quiz: teacher calls out pairs of denominators, students call back the LCD. "4 and 6?" "12!" "5 and 8?" "40!" Build speed and confidence.

Model Drawing — Multi-Step Problems (20 min)

2-step word problems. Teacher models, then students try:

"A tank was 5/6 full of water. 1/4 of the water was used for washing. What fraction of the tank still had water?"

Method: Read → Draw bar model → Identify BOTH operations → Solve step by step → Check.

Group Challenge (15 min)

Problem-solving in groups of 4. Each group gets an advanced problem (Olympiad-style). Groups work on mini-whiteboards, then present solution to class. Applaud all attempts.

Consolidation (10 min)

Students self-assess using traffic light: 🟢 green (confident), 🟡 amber (mostly ok, some gaps), 🔴 red (need more help). Teacher notes who is red/amber for Friday's differentiated groups.

Homework

Mixed practice worksheet — addition AND subtraction, computation and word problems

Lesson 5 — Assessment and Mastery

Friday | 50 minutes | Objective: Assess understanding; extend and support

Mini-Assessment (20 min)

10-question quiz covering all skills from the week. Mix of computation (6 questions) and word problems (4 questions). Students work individually in silence. Collect papers.

Differentiated Follow-Up (25 min)

Based on Thursday's traffic light AND Friday's quiz (quick-mark during the 25 min):

Mastery
Extension: adding/subtracting 3 fractions, fraction puzzles, challenge problems from Maths Olympiad bank. These students may also peer-tutor.
Consolidation
Rework errors from quiz. Practise 5 more examples with bar model support. Teacher checks work before they move to extension.
Support
Guided small group with teacher. Return to fraction manipulatives, focus on LCD finding. Step-by-step worked examples with scaffolding.

Wrap-Up (5 min)

Preview next week: multiplication of fractions. Teaser question: "If adding fractions means finding common pieces, what do you think multiplying fractions might mean? Have a think over the weekend!"

Homework

Quiz papers sent home for parent signature. Students below 60% receive a targeted practice sheet for the weekend.

Assessment Notes

Quiz results recorded in markbook. Students below 60% flagged for intervention next week (before multiplication begins). Share results with parents via quiz paper return.
