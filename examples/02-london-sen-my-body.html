Session 1: Head & Face

Monday | 20 minutes

Sensory Warm-Up (2 min)

Gentle face massage with hands: forehead, cheeks, chin. Teacher models, students copy. Use a mirror so they can watch themselves.

Main Activity (13 min)

Large face outline on A3 paper (pre-drawn). Students add features: eyes, nose, mouth, ears, hair.

Teacher points to own face, names each part, students repeat. Use Makaton signs alongside spoken words.

Emerging
Point to named body part on request (e.g., "show me your nose"). Physical prompt if needed.
Developing
Match label cards to face parts with adult support. Use Makaton signs as prompts.
Secure
Label face parts independently using word cards or writing.
Extension
Describe what each part does: "Eyes are for seeing. Ears are for hearing."

Wind-Down (5 min)

Soft music. Students lie down or sit comfortably. Teacher gently names body parts while students rest hands on each one: "Touch your head... touch your ears..."

Resources

  • A3 face outlines (pre-drawn, one per student)
  • Crayons, glue sticks, collage materials (wool for hair, buttons for eyes)
  • Body part label cards with Makaton symbols
  • Small mirrors (one per pair)
  • Calm music playlist

Staff Briefing

Behaviour strategies: Use "now and next" board throughout. Give 2-minute transition warnings before moving between phases. If a student becomes dysregulated during the face-touching warm-up (some may find face contact difficult), offer alternative: squeeze a stress ball or press hands together instead.

1:1 focus: Support emerging-level students with hand-over-hand for the craft activity if needed. Keep instructions to 1-step at a time.

Session 2: Arms & Hands

Tuesday | 20 minutes

Sensory Warm-Up (2 min)

Hand massage with unscented lotion. Students rub hands together, squeeze fingers one by one. Count fingers together: "One, two, three..."

Main Activity (13 min)

Hand-print painting! Students press painted hands onto paper. Label: hand, fingers, thumb, arm, elbow, wrist.

Pair activity: students high-five, then point to and name each part on their partner's arm.

Emerging
Participate in hand-print activity with support. Respond to "show me your hand" with physical gesture.
Developing
Name 3+ parts (hand, arm, fingers) with visual support. Match labels to hand-print.
Secure
Label all parts independently. Begin to describe: "I use my hands to..."
Extension
"What can you do with your hands?" — list 5+ actions (write, draw, wave, hold, clap).

Wind-Down (5 min)

"Shake it out" — shake hands fast for 10 seconds, then slowly let them go floppy. Repeat 3 times, getting slower each time. End with hands resting in lap, eyes closed, 30 seconds of quiet.

Resources

  • Washable paint (skin-safe), shallow trays
  • A3 paper, wet wipes, aprons
  • Body part label cards with Makaton symbols
  • Unscented lotion for warm-up

Staff Briefing

Sensory alert: Some students may resist paint on hands. Have sponge stamps as an alternative. Offer plastic gloves if needed — participation matters more than method.

Transition: Have wet wipes ready IMMEDIATELY after painting — students who dislike messy hands need to clean up fast or they'll escalate.

Session 3: Legs & Feet

Wednesday | 20 minutes

Sensory Warm-Up (2 min)

Marching on the spot! Then stomping on different textures: carpet square, rubber mat, bubble wrap. "How does it feel? Hard? Soft? Bumpy?"

Main Activity (13 min)

Draw around feet on large paper (shoes off). Decorate foot outlines. Label: foot, toes, heel, ankle, leg, knee.

Movement game: "Simon Says... touch your knee! Stamp your foot! Wiggle your toes!"

Emerging
Point to body part on request. Participate in Simon Says with physical prompts.
Developing
Match label to body part with support. Follow 2-step Simon Says instructions.
Secure
Write labels independently. Lead a round of Simon Says for the group.
Extension
Compare foot sizes. "Whose foot is biggest?" Introduce measurement language.

Wind-Down (5 min)

Foot massage with a tennis ball — roll the ball under feet while sitting. Calm music playing. Transition to shoes back on slowly.

Resources

  • Large paper, crayons, stickers
  • Texture squares: carpet, rubber mat, bubble wrap
  • Body part label cards with Makaton symbols
  • Tennis balls (one per student) for wind-down

Staff Briefing

Shoes off: Some students may be uncomfortable removing shoes. Don't force it — they can draw around their shoe instead. Respect the boundary.

Bubble wrap: The popping noise may overstimulate some students. Have ear defenders available or offer it as a choice activity rather than whole-group.

Session 4: My Senses

Thursday | 20 minutes

Sensory Warm-Up (2 min)

"Sensory wake-up": close eyes and listen (what can you hear?), smell something (cinnamon stick or orange peel), feel something (feather, fabric, sandpaper).

Main Activity (13 min)

Five senses stations — rotate in pairs every 2-3 minutes:

  • See: Kaleidoscope or colour paddles
  • Hear: Shakers with different fillings (rice, buttons, sand)
  • Touch: Feely bag — reach in, describe, then look
  • Smell: Scent jars (vanilla, lemon, mint, cinnamon)
  • Taste: Tiny samples — sweet (raisin), sour (lemon drop), salty (pretzel)

After stations: "Which body part do we use to see? To hear?" Link sense to body part.

Emerging
Participate in sensory exploration. Show preference (reach toward liked item, turn away from disliked).
Developing
Name 3+ senses. Match sense to body part with picture support.
Secure
Name all 5 senses and their body parts. Describe what they experienced at each station.
Extension
"What would happen if we couldn't see?" Discussion about accessibility and helping others.

Wind-Down (5 min)

Quiet listening activity: lie down, eyes closed, teacher plays 5 different sounds (bell, rain stick, clap, whisper, music box). Students count how many they heard.

Resources

  • Scent jars, feely bag with objects, shakers, taste samples
  • Kaleidoscope or colour paddles
  • Five senses picture cards with Makaton symbols
  • Sound instruments for wind-down (bell, rain stick, music box)

Staff Briefing

Allergies: CHECK BEFORE THE SESSION — no nuts, check all taste samples against allergy list. If in doubt, skip the taste station entirely.

Sensory overload: Five stations may be too much for some students. Have a "quiet corner" available where they can sit with one item at a time. Let them opt in and out.

EHCP links: This session links well to communication targets (describing experiences) and sensory processing targets (tolerating new textures/smells).

Session 5: All of Me!

Friday | 20 minutes

Sensory Warm-Up (2 min)

"Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes" song — do it twice, once fast and once sloooow. Lots of laughter encouraged.

Main Activity (13 min)

Life-size body outline: Each student lies on a large sheet of paper while a partner (or adult) draws around them. Then they fill in: face, hands, feet, and label as many body parts as they can.

Display on the wall — "This is ME!"

Celebration circle: Each student shares one thing they learned this week about their body. Every contribution gets applause.

Emerging
Tolerate lying on paper (or stand against wall for outline). Point to 3+ body parts when named.
Developing
Add features to outline with support. Name 5+ body parts. Share one thing in the circle.
Secure
Label outline independently. Describe body parts and their functions. Confident sharing in the circle.
Extension
"My body can..." — write or dictate 5 things their body helps them do. Link to self-esteem.

Wind-Down (5 min)

Breathing exercise: "Smell the flower" (breathe in through nose), "Blow out the candle" (breathe out through mouth). Repeat 5 times. Calm music. End with: "You did amazing this week. Your body is brilliant!"

Resources

  • Large paper rolls (big enough for full body outlines)
  • Crayons, markers, collage materials
  • All body part label cards from the week
  • Blu-Tack for wall display
  • Calm music playlist

Staff Briefing

Lying on paper: Some students may find this uncomfortable or frightening (being drawn around). Offer standing against the wall as an alternative, or use a smaller paper for just hand/foot outlines.

Assessment: This is your formative assessment opportunity for the week. Observe and note which body parts each student can identify/name. Update EHCP progress notes accordingly.

Celebration: Make the sharing circle feel safe. No one is forced to speak — thumbs up or pointing to their picture counts as sharing. Every response gets the same applause.
